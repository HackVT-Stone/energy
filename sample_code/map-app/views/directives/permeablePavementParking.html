Permeable pavement systems are hardscape materials that allow liquids to infiltrate through void spaces in the material, or through open spaces between components of the material, while maintaining the functionality of an impervious surface. These systems, whether the trafficked surface is made of porous asphalt, porous concrete, or pavers, are underlain by an aggregate base that provides structural support, runoff storage, and pollutant removal. Permeable pavement is well suited for parking lots, walking paths, sidewalks, driveways, low speed-low volume lanes, plazas, and low vehicle weight streets.

Properly installed and maintained permeable pavement has a significant life-span, and existing systems that are more than twenty years in age continue to function. Because water drains through the surface course and into the subsurface bed, freeze-thaw cycles do not tend to adversely affect these pavement systems.

See www.vtwaterquality.org/stormwater/htm/sw_manualrevision.htm for details and current status of this effort. The treatment volume and crediting of permeable pavement towards meeting the applicable stormwater management goals in the new manual is likely to differ slightly from the inputs provided above, which do not account for the permeability of underlying soils.
