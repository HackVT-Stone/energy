Rain gardens are typically surface depressions with native plantings and amended soils used to capture, slow, infiltrate, and treat runoff from impervious surfaces, including rooftops. The amended soil can be underlain by a layer of gravel aggregate to store and infiltrate additional runoff. Stormwater from a disconnected roof downspout collects in the bioretention practice and infiltrates into the soil with the help of the long-rooted native plants. Bioretention practices are generally designed to pond to depths of 6-18 inches, with a complete drawdown in 48 hours. They provide volume control, groundwater recharge, moderate peak flows and provide an aesthetic alternative to a traditional stormwater pond.

This calculator uses default depths of 18 inches of amended soil substrate and 12 inches of gravel aggregate for bioretention facilities.
