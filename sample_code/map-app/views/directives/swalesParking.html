Dry swales are gently sloped open channels vegetated with native plants that reduce runoff volumes through infiltration and evapotranspiration. This category of swale is located in a parking lot to absorb runoff from the lot. Dry swales can absorb low flows, and carry runoff from heavy rains to storm sewer inlets or directly to surface waters. These practices improve water quality by infiltrating the first flush of storm water runoff and filtering the large storm flows they convey.

This calculator uses a default combined depth of 24 inches of amended soil and aggregate.

Use the “Percentage of Parking Lot Runoff Captured by Dry Swale” slider bar to automatically adjust the area of the swale to capture different amounts of the runoff from your parking area, or adjust the specific design values below to determine the area and depth of the swale.
